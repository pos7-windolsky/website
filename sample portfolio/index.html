Explore Misamis Occidental

Misamis Occidental officially the Province of Misamis Occidental, is a province located in the region of Northern Mindanao in the Philippines. Its capital is the city of Oroquieta while Ozamiz is the most-populous city. The province borders Zamboanga del Norte and Zamboanga del Sur to the west and is separated from Lanao del Norte by Panguil Bay to the south and Iligan Bay to the east.

Tourist Spots

Did you know?

  • 🐊 Misamis Occidental Aquamarine Park (MOAP) in Sinacaban is famous for its crocodile farm and dolphin rescue center.
  • 🏝️ The province has hidden islands like Naawan Island and Baobawon Island, perfect for eco-tourism.
  • 🛡️ The old name of Ozamiz was Misamis, from the Subanen word kuyamis (a kind of coconut).
  • 🎭 The Subanen Courtship Dance is unique—it mimics birds courting each other.

  • 🚂 In the Spanish period, Ozamiz was once envisioned as a trade hub with a railway system linking Mindanao ports.
  • 🌴 Misamis Occidental is a top producer of coconuts and bananas in Northern Mindanao.
  • 🔔 The Ozamiz Cathedral Bell is one of the oldest in Mindanao, cast in the late 1800s.
  • 🐢 Turtle nesting sites can still be found along some coastal areas of the province.

    Municipality of Clarin

    Tourist Spot : RA7 Medina Waters Beach Resort

    The municipality of Clarin is located in the province of Misamis Occidental, Northern Mindanao, Philippines. It is a coastal municipality with a 2024 population of 39,867, and its economy is primarily based on agriculture and fishing, with significant agricultural land and fishery resources in Panguil Bay.

    Municipality of Tudela

    Tourist Spot : Subanen Village

    The municipality of Tudela is a 4th class municipality located in the province of Misamis Occidental, Philippines, with a population of 28,599 people as of the 2020 census. It is bordered by Sinacaban to the north, Clarin to the south, Iligan Bay to the east, and the municipalities of Don Victoriano and the Mount Malindang range to the west. Tudela is known for its Binalbal Festival and has received awards for its Child-Friendly Local Governance and its commitment to good governance.

    Municipality of Sinacaban

    Tourist Spot : La.Elena Fishyalan Aquapark

    The municipality of Sinacaban is located in the province of Misamis Occidental, within the Northern Mindanao region of the Philippines. It is a coastal town with a total land area of 99.09 square kilometers and a population of 19,671 as of the 2020 census. Sinacaban is politically divided into 17 barangays.

    Municipality of Aloran

    Tourist Spot : Kahakai Resort

    The Municipality of Aloran is a local government unit in the province of Misamis Occidental, Philippines, with ZIP code 7206. Aloran is one of the 14 municipalities in the province and is known for its diverse native languages, including Subanon, Cebuano, and Tagalog.

    Municipality of Lopez Jaena

    Tourist Spot : Lucel’s Beach Resort

    TLopez Jaena, officially the Municipality of Lopez Jaena (Cebuano: Lungsod sa Lopez Jaena; Subanen: Benwa D' Lopez Jaena; Tagalog: Bayan ng Lopez Jaena), is a municipality in the province of Misamis Occidental, Philippines and a suburb of neighboring Oroquieta City.

    Municipality of Plaridel

    Tourist Spot : Defiesta Family Beach Resort

    The Municipality of Plaridel is located in the province of Misamis Occidental, Northern Mindanao, Philippines. It is a 3rd-class municipality that was formerly known as Langaran before its name was changed by Philippine Legislature Act No. 2390 in 1914. As of the 2020 Census, it had a population of 39,840 people and comprises 33 barangays. The current mayor of Plaridel is Gadwin E. Handumon.

    Municipality of Baliangao

    Tourist Spot : Sam Niza Beach Resort

    Baliangao is a coastal municipality in the province of Misamis Occidental, Philippines, known for its white sand beaches and as a significant seafood producer. With a land area of 81.72 square kilometers, it features 15 politically divided barangays (villages) and attractions such as the natural beauty of the U River.

    Municipality of Calamba

    Tourist Spot : Fil Claire Resort

    Calamba is a municipality in the province of Misamis Occidental, Northern Mindanao, Philippines, with a population of 23,227 as of the 2020 Census. Previously known as Solinog, the municipality was officially established on February 1, 1948, and is known for its agricultural activities, particularly with coconut and cassava.

    Municipality of Sapang Dalaga

    Tourist Spot : Caluya Shrine

    Sapang Dalaga is a 5th class municipality in the province of Misamis Occidental, Philippines. Its name is a historical Visayan phrase meaning "maiden in a creek" and it has a land area of 93.93 square kilometers. The municipality includes several barangays such as Boundary and Caluya.
